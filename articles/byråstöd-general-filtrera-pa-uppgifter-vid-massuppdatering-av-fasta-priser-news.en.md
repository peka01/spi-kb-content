# Better control when adjusting your fixed prices in {{resource:Common_Product_Name_Variable_Advisor}}

Now you can be even more precise when it's time to adjust your prices in Spiris. We have listened to your requests and added a filter for specific tasks in the guide for mass updating fixed prices. This means you no longer have to search through long lists when, for example, you only want to increase the price for year-end closing or another specific service. By filtering for exactly what you need, you get a better overview and can feel confident that the correct prices are being updated.

In the first step of the guide, you select the specific task you want to focus on by using the new filter. You can easily search for or select the task from the dropdown list to narrow down your selection and make it more manageable.

Once you have made your selection, the list is automatically updated to only show the fixed-price models where the selected task is included. It is important to note that even if a fixed price consists of a package with several different tasks (for example, both "Ongoing bookkeeping" and "VAT reporting"), the entire fixed price will be included in the selection as long as the selected task is part of the content. This makes it possible to smoothly adjust the whole for the affected packages.

**Examples of how you can use the filter:**

*   **Targeted price adjustments:** If you have decided to increase the price for the "Payroll" service, you can filter for all fixed prices that contain that specific task. You can then carry out the price change for these specific subscriptions in one go, without affecting subscriptions that only contain other services.
*   **Updating for changed conditions:** If a specific task, such as "Year-end closing", requires more resources than before, you can quickly identify all customers who have this included in their fixed price and adjust the pricing accordingly.

This workflow saves valuable time and reduces the risk of manual errors. It gives you full control over your revenue and allows you to make precise adjustments to specific parts of your offering without affecting the rest of your subscriptions or customer agreements.
